Samy spent more than a decade working in the supply chains that move consumer goods from Asia to the US.

Bangkok. Hong Kong. Shanghai. Not on calls — in person, inside the factories.

What he kept seeing: American retail and wholesale brands — some big, most mid-sized — getting burned by sourcing arrangements that looked good on paper. Agents who worked over email. Factories recommended by brokers who'd never set foot in them. Quality checks that happened (maybe) a few days before a container sailed, by someone the buyer had never met.

The goods would land. And something would be off. Color, finish, stitching, print registration, packaging. Sometimes small things. Sometimes the kind of thing that cost a full PO.

What Namu is

Namu is Samy's answer to that pattern. A sourcing and QA consultancy built around one non-negotiable: physical presence. In every facility. At every stage of production.

Namu's target is American retail and wholesale brands sourcing printed and branded goods — apparel, promotional items, custom packaging, branded hardware. The work is the same whether the order is 5,000 units or 50,000: vet the supplier in person, approve the sample, audit the production line, inspect the finished goods, and sign off on the shipment before it moves.

Why the four cities matter

Namu operates across Bangkok, Hong Kong, Shanghai, and California. Each location is deliberate.

  • Bangkok: apparel, soft goods, leather, and increasingly competitive on branded hardware as Thailand absorbs manufacturing moving out of China.
  • Hong Kong: the financial and logistical hub — where deals get structured and shipments get coordinated.
  • Shanghai: the industrial backbone for larger-volume runs, complex printing, and packaging.
  • California: the US-side presence for client management, time zone coverage, and end-of-line quality verification.

The point isn't the list of cities. The point is that at every choke point in your supply chain, there's a Namu person there in real time.

How Samy works

Samy personally handles every client relationship. That's a feature, not a scaling limitation — Namu's value is that you get a real person who knows your product, your tolerances, and your timelines. Not a rotating account manager.

Engagements typically start with a 20-minute discovery call. If Namu is the right fit for what you're sourcing, the next step is a sourcing brief — specs, quantity, target cost, timeline.

From there: supplier identification, sample production, pricing negotiation, pre-production review, inline QA, and final inspection. One invoice. One point of contact. End to end.
